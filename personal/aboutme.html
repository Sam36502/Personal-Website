"What do you look like?"

I'm not really comfortable putting images of myself on the internet, but I'm also not so interested as to stop others if they take a picture of me and post it somewhere. I'm an obese, brown-haired, green-eyed mass of flesh and bones. I'm trying, and failing, to grow a beard, which means I just have that pathetic juvenile bum-fluff under my nose. I walk awkwardly and stand in an equally unappealing way, on account of the extra mass I've been porting my whole life. I would describe my general appearance as unkempt, but hey: at least I shower every day, so I have one up on those insolent mummy's boys that go to anime conventions and hold beliefs only shared by the extreme or archaic.

"The mind of such vulgar barbarities burgeons only with the thought of bringing anguish upon those who do not deserve it!"

"What kind of stuff do you like?"

Sadly, I have very few pronounced interests and those are not at all very pronounced. I like to read, but not sometimes. I like to play games, but often can't decide what to play or don't feel like playing at all after a while. Probably the most consistant thing I like is talking about philosophy with friends and family. I also like programming. That's why I made it my job. Apart from that, there are thousands of disciplines I picked up for less than a day believing I could become the best over night and lost interest in. Some of them recur enough for me to give them more regular attention, like calligraphy (Both European and Asian) or playing the piano. I'm hoping to find the thing that I could do forever without losing interest and make it my job, but --- at the moment, at least --- that seems utterly impossible.

"No creature of such off-putting stature and manner could contain any breath of life than that of the souls of valiant but foolish men."

"What are your general beliefs?"

I'd like to say I'm Agnostic, but I have faith in Atheism. I don't know why; I just think it's the most comforting religion, and seems correct, but I can't prove it at all. I have no problem with Religious people until they endanger others. Politically, I'm about as centrist as it gets. That normally manifests itself in the form of others with opinions claiming I'm part of "The Enemy". I try to implement the Stoicism of the ancient Greeks in my day-to-day life, with varying degrees of success. At least it's made me content with the idea of death, so that's a bonus.
