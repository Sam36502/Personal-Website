About Me

So you want to know about me? Well, there's not much to know.
For one thing, I really don't like writing about myself, but it feels appropriate for a personal website to write something about me here so, here's the summary:

"Where are you from?"

I was born in Switzerland, raised in New Zealand, England and then mostly in Switzerland, but somehow I feel more British than anything. I can speak English and German (as well as Swiss-German) and now Toki Pona. I prefer to speak English where possible as I'm more able to articulate what I want to say, but regularly use German in my day-to-day life as well. My English accent is kinda weird as it's pretty much a mix of lots of different ones, but I think I'm pretty decent at imitating accents, so I guess you could say it's anything I want.

"What kind of stuff do you like?"

Sadly, I have very few pronounced interests and those are not at all very pronounced. I like to read, but not sometimes. I like to play games, but often can't decide what to play or don't feel like playing at all after a while. Probably the most consistant thing I like is talking about philosophy with friends and family. I also like programming. That's why I made it my job. Apart from that, there are thousands of disciplines I picked up for less than a day believing I could become the best over night and lost interest in. Some of them reemerge enough for me to give them more regular attention, like calligraphy (Both European and Asian) or playing the piano. I'm hoping to find the thing that I could do forever without losing interest and make it my job, but --- at the moment, at least --- programming is something I'm really passionate about and find generally fulfilling as a job.
