Links I Often Forget

These are some links to useful websites I always seem to forget the name of when I need them most, or that I just use so frequently, it seems ridiculous to have to google their names every time.
As usual, they're in no particular order.
(Don't worry about "open in new tab"-ing, they do that automatically)

Languages

Anglo-Saxon - Old English

  • Old English Translator — Old English searchable dictionary with inflection tables
  • Bosworth-Toller — Old English searchable dictionary with good examples

Conlanging & Linguistics

  • WALS — The World Atlas of Language Structures is a large database of structural properties of languages
  • PHOIBLE — repository of cross-linguistic phonological inventory data

Research

  • Sci-Hub — A website for getting research papers for free

Design

  • Coolors — Page that lets you generate good-looking colour palettes
  • Clrs.cc — Website with better colour palette for websites
  • Google Font Picker — Font-Picker with nice, web-compatible fonts
  • HTML 5 UP! — A bunch of actually well-made webpage templates that are CC-BY
  • LaTeX Cheatsheet — Some images with common LaTeX commands listed

IT / Computer Stuff

  • Shapecatcher — A website to draw a unicode character and get ones that fit it best.
  • Crontab Guru — A website for picking crontab times.
