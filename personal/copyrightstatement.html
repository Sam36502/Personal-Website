My Copyright Policy

In my opinion, copyright nowadays lasts far too long. (70 years after creator's death, in most countries) I see no benefit to the public with this system; it exists because of, and for large companies to keep exclusive money-making rights on intellectual property. The result is the hindrance of creative progress. In an effort to protest this, I make following statement.

Copyright Statement:

I, Samuel Christophe Oscar Pearce, being of sound mind, do hereby declare that every piece of copyrightable material I produce in my lifetime shall enter the public domain no later than 30 years after first publication. In the event that I die while still owner of some intellectual property ("the IP"), I hereby grant it to my parents and/or brother for joint owenership. If they do not decide when the IP should be enter public domain, it is automatically made part of the public domain one year exactly after the date of my death.

Disclaimer:

I'm not a lawyer and don't know if this one statement will even be enough, but I do intent to attach a license to anything I might publish to clarify exactly when I revoke my copyright on it. I'm really only making this statement to make clear my stance, on the matter as a whole, and to hopefully clear things up, should I die unexpectedly and leave something un-public-domained.
